### 1. Coulomb's Law
**Problem:** Four point charges of $+1.0 \text{ C}$ each are placed at the corners of a square with sides of $1.0 \text{ m}$. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the electric force on a charge of $-2.0 \text{ C}$ placed at the center of the square.

**Solution:**
* The distance from each corner to the center of the square is $r = \frac{\sqrt{1.0^2 + 1.0^2}}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707 \text{ m}$.
* The force exerted by one corner charge $q_c = +1.0 \text{ C}$ on the center charge $q_0 = -2.0 \text{ C}$ is given by $F = \frac{k |q_c q_0|}{r^2}$.
* Due to the square's symmetry, the force from the top-left corner is equal in magnitude and opposite in direction to the force from the bottom-right corner.
* These opposing force vectors cancel each other out: $\sum \vec{F} = 0$.
* **Result:** The net electric force is $0 \text{ N}$.
